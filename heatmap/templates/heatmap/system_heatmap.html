Ratio of Available Bikes to Station Capacity

Note: Please move the fps slider to the right to get a more useful animation.

This map shows how many bikes are available at each station relative to the total capacity of the station. So a station with a capacity of 20 docks having 10 available bikes has the same heat intensity as a station with 10 docks having 5 available bikes.

This means that this map shows bike availability relative to the available infrastructure in the area.

This map represent data from Dec 06 2017 from 00:00 to 24:00 taken in 5-minute increments.

Total Number of Bikes Per-Station

Note: Please move the fps slider to the right to get a more useful animation.

This map is a bit simpler in that it just shows the number of bikes available at each station. So a station with 20 docks having 5 available bikes has the same heat intensity as a station with 10 docks having 5 available bikes.

The catch with this map is that there are areas with far more bikes available, such as Bay/Front, which will almost always show peak intensity. This would be more useful if the bikeshare infrastructure was spread out more evenly geographically.

This map represent data from Dec 06 2017 from 00:00 to 24:00 taken in 5-minute increments.

How this works

The stations and their locations are pulled from the station_information endpoint of the BikeShare Toronto Open Data API. The stations' total capacity and number of bikes available is pulled from the station_status endpoint
